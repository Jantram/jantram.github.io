Dr. Ritesh Sarkhel

Assistant Professor · Computer Science and Engineering, IIT Kharagpur

Jantram is an AI research group at IIT Kharagpur focused on Agentic AI, Natural Language Processing, and Applied Machine Learning. We build systems that understand language, reason over complex inputs, and act autonomously in real-world environments. Our work spans foundational research and practical tools, with the goal of making intelligent systems more capable, interpretable, and reliable.

Jun 2025 Publication

AgentTrace accepted at ACL 2025

Our paper "AgentTrace: Interpretable Reasoning Chains for Multi-Step Language Agents" was accepted at the Annual Meeting of the ACL. The work introduces a framework for faithful chain-of-thought generation in long-horizon agentic tasks. ACL, Vienna, July 2025.

May 2025 Award

Best Paper Honorable Mention at NAACL 2025

Our work on structured information extraction from heterogeneous scientific documents received an Honorable Mention at NAACL 2025. Albuquerque, NM.

Apr 2025 New Members

Two new PhD students joining Fall 2025

Welcome to Priya Mehta, who will work on tool-augmented reasoning, and Samuel Osei, who will focus on low-resource NLP for African languages. Both join in August 2025.

Mar 2025 Invited Talk

Talk at MIT NLP Colloquium

Dr. Sarkhel delivered an invited talk on "Towards Reliable Autonomous Agents" at the MIT NLP Colloquium, covering our recent work on robust planning and uncertainty-aware decision-making in LLM agents.

Feb 2025 Publication

Paper accepted at ICLR 2025

"Compositional Generalization in Instruction-Following Agents via Hierarchical Task Decomposition" will appear at ICLR 2025, Singapore. We show that hierarchical planning significantly improves zero-shot compositional generalization.
